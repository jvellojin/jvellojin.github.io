Hi, I'm Jesús Vellojin, a Colombian mathematician working on numerical analysis and computational modeling, areas applied to problems of solid mechanics, fluids and viscoelastic structures. I got my math degree at [Universidad de Cartagena](https://www.unicartagena.edu.co). I have a master degree in math from [Universidad del Norte](https://www.uninorte.edu.co), Barranquilla, Colombia. I got my PhD through a consortium of Chilean universities: [Universidad Técnica Federico Santa María](https://usm.cl), [Pontificia Universidad Católica de Valparaíso](https://www.pucv.cl) and [Universidad de Valparaíso](https://uv.cl).

I am currently an Assistant Professor at the [Departamento de Ciencias, Universidad Técnica Federico Santa María](https://www.usm.cl) in Valparaíso, Chile. My research focuses on the numerical solution of partial differential equations, mixed formulations, and locking-free methods for slender structures, with applications in water desalination, elasticity problems, fluid-flow problems, coupled problems, and eigenvalue problems.

In my free time I enjoy watching YouTube science channels, playing chess, learning new programming languages and cooking. I enjoy Chilean food very much and I have learned to make fusion meals where I mix the best of Chile and Colombia.
